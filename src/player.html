In this tutorial, you will learn the source filter theory as it pertains to the following three vowel sounds:

"ee" / i / as in bee "ah" / a / as in pot "oo" / u / as in boo

Human vowel sounds are produced with a. Laryngeal Source (voicing): the sound wave produced by the vibrating vocal folds.

complex periodic waveform

simple periodic waveform

Human vowel sounds are produced with:
a. Laryngeal Source (voicing): the sound wave produced by the vibrating vocal folds and
b. a 'filter': the process whereby the vocal tract modifies the sound of the vibrating vocal folds

The result of the filtering of the vocal tract is that the simple sinusoid waveform of the vibrating vocal folds is changed into a complex waveform. We hear this complex waveform that is radiated out of the mouth of the speaker as the different vowel sounds.

"ah" / a / as in pot
Here is the second sound presented. It is the "ah" sound as in the word "pot." Notice, now, to formulate the "ah" sound, the tongue lowers and moves back. The oral cavity becomes larger and the pharyngeal cavity becomes smaller; so F2 gets lower and F1 gets higher. The harmonics remain the same because the vocal folds are vibrating at the same rate.
